## Mistake 2 — Insufficient Or Wrong Data

What distinguishes "insufficient data" from "wrong data," and why does each undermine analysis?

---

- **Insufficient data** — not enough records to draw statistically reliable conclusions; small samples produce unstable results.
- **Wrong data** — data that does not represent the target population, even if the sample size is large. For example, surveying mostly young adults when the research question is about elderly people produces a biased dataset that cannot answer the intended question.

Both issues produce findings that appear valid but are actually unreliable.
