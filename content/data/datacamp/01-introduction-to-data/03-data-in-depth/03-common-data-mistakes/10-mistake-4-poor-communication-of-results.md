## Mistake 4 — Poor Communication Of Results

Why is poor communication of results considered especially costly, and what are its two main consequences?

---

Poor communication renders the entire preceding work — data collection, cleaning, and analysis — **worthless in practice**, since findings that are not understood cannot be acted upon. Its two main consequences are:
1. **Wasted effort** — insights that exist but are never used.
2. **Misunderstandings or incorrect conclusions** — stakeholders may misinterpret results and make poor decisions based on them.
