## Lack Of Context In Analysis

Researchers observe a steep decline in contactless payments and conclude that elderly people are less inclined to use this payment method. What is wrong with this conclusion?

---

The researchers failed to account for **data context**. Unknown to them, widespread technical issues with payment terminals during that week were the actual cause of the decline — not a behavioral preference among elderly people. Without investigating the broader circumstances surrounding the data, they misattributed an external factor to a demographic trend.

This illustrates how lack of context leads to incorrect conclusions even from accurate data.
