## How Planning Ahead Prevents Data Mistakes

How does investing more time in the planning stage of the data life cycle reduce the likelihood of common data mistakes?

---

Thorough planning addresses the root cause of most data mistakes before they occur:
- A clearly defined question prevents vague data collection and misaligned analysis.
- Identifying the target population in advance reduces sampling bias.
- Choosing analysis methods upfront ensures they suit the data type and research question.
- Planning how to present findings to specific stakeholders improves communication from the start.

Most downstream problems in a data project can be traced back to insufficient planning.
