## Communication Mistake — Cherry-Picking And Misleading Charts

What is cherry-picking in data communication, and why is it a serious ethical problem?

---

Cherry-picking means **selectively presenting only the data points that support a desired conclusion** while ignoring contradicting evidence. It is a serious ethical problem because:
1. It misrepresents what the data actually shows.
2. It can lead stakeholders to make decisions based on a false picture.
3. It violates the principles of transparency and honest data use.

Using misleading chart types — such as truncated axes or distorted scales — has the same effect and is equally problematic.
