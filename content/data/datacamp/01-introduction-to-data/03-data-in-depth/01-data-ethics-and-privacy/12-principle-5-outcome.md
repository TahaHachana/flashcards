## Principle 5 — Outcome

What does the outcome principle of data ethics require, and why is it necessary even when intentions are good?

---

The outcome principle requires organizations to **monitor the real-world results of their data use** and ensure it does not cause harm — even unintentionally. Good intentions do not guarantee good outcomes. Data use can inadvertently harm individuals or vulnerable populations in ways that were not foreseen, making ongoing monitoring and willingness to adjust essential.
