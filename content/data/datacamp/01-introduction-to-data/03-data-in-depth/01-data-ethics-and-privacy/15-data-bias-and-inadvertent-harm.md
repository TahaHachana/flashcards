## Data Bias And Inadvertent Harm

How does data bias connect to the outcome principle of data ethics?

---

Data bias refers to systematic errors in data collection, sampling, or measurement that make a dataset unrepresentative — leading to misleading or unfair conclusions. Even with good intentions, a model or decision built on biased data can cause **inadvertent harm**: for example, a loan algorithm trained on historically biased data may unfairly disadvantage certain groups.

This is why the outcome principle requires active monitoring — bias-driven harm often only becomes visible after deployment, not during design.
