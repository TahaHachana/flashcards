## Principle 2 — Transparency

What does the transparency principle require, and what are the consequences of failing to uphold it?

---

Transparency requires organizations to be **open with users about how their data will be collected, stored, and used** — giving individuals the information they need to make an informed choice about whether to share their data.

Failing to be transparent exposes organizations to:
1. **Reputation damage** — when users discover their data is not being used as expected.
2. **Legal issues** — regulatory frameworks increasingly mandate transparency in data use.
