## Correlation Vs Causation

What is the difference between correlation and causation, and why does it matter during the analysis and decision steps?

---

- **Correlation** — a statistical relationship where two variables change together, but neither necessarily causes the other.
- **Causation** — one variable directly causes changes in another.

The two must never be assumed equivalent. During analysis, mistaking correlation for causation can produce a confident-looking but fundamentally wrong insight — leading to decisions that act on a false cause. For example, concluding that a marketing campaign *caused* a sales spike when both were actually driven by a third factor (e.g., seasonality).
