Hardware–Software Symbiosis

What is meant by hardware–software symbiosis?

Hardware is useless without software; software is useless without hardware—they depend on each other.

Hardware Categories

What are the two broad categories of computer hardware components?

Internal components and external components (peripherals).

Peripheral Functional Categories

What are the three functional categories of peripherals?

Input devices, output devices, and specialty devices.

Define Peripheral

What is a peripheral?

An external device that sends data to or receives data from a computer.

Common Input Devices

List five common input devices.

Keyboard, mouse, digital camera, scanner, microphone.

Common Output Devices

List four common output devices.

Monitor, printer, speakers, 3D printer.

Specialty Devices Examples

Give four examples of specialty devices.

Storage drives, barcode readers, digital pens, networking devices.

Keyboard Purpose

What is the primary purpose of a keyboard?

Input letters, numbers, symbols, and commands.

QWERTY Layout

What is the most common keyboard layout in the United States?

QWERTY.

Name two wired connection types historically used for keyboards and mice.

USB Type-A and PS/2.

Mouse Function

What device controls a cursor to interact with on‑screen objects?

A mouse.

What enables touch input on screens?

A touch-sensitive overlay that detects location.

Webcam Purpose

What is a webcam used for?

Recording or streaming video.

Webcam Connections

Scanner Function

What does a scanner do?

Converts paper documents or images into digital data.

Microphone Function

What is the main function of a microphone?

Capturing audio for input.

Video Connection Types

List four common video connection types for monitors/projectors.

VGA, DVI, HDMI, DisplayPort.

Speakers Function

What do speakers do?

Convert electrical audio signals into sound.

Printer Purpose

What is the purpose of a printer?

Produce hard copies of digital documents or images.

Printer Selection Criteria

List five criteria to consider when selecting a printer.

Print quality, size options, capacity, speed, cost per page.

Inkjet Process

How does an inkjet printer produce images?

Sprays tiny dots of liquid ink onto paper.

Inkjet Disadvantages

Give two disadvantages of inkjet printers.

Expensive ink; ink can bleed, fade, or run when wet.

Laser Disadvantages

List two disadvantages of laser printers.

Higher purchase cost; harder maintenance.

What printing process do 3D printers use?

Additive layering of material from a 3D model.

Laser Network Suitability

Why are laser printers often better for network sharing?

Inkjet Bleed

Why does inkjet output sometimes look less sharp?

Ink can bleed into paper fibers.

Toner Durability

Why is toner output more durable than inkjet output?

Heat-fused toner doesn’t dissolve or fade easily.

Specialty vs Standard I/O

What differentiates a specialty device from standard I/O?

It performs a specific role (e.g., biometric, networking, storage).

What configuration tools exist for keyboards and mice?

Why choose an inkjet despite higher ink costs?

What printer type is best for high-volume documents?

Laser printer.

3D vs Traditional Printing

Builds physical objects layer by layer.

External hard drive; USB flash drive.
