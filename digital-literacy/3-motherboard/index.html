Motherboard Role

What is the motherboard’s overall role in a computer?

It acts as the central nervous system, providing connectors and circuits to link devices to the CPU.

Bus Definition

What do buses on a motherboard do?

They are small groups of wires that connect different devices and carry data or power.

Bus Width

What is bus width and how is it measured?

The number of wires in a bus, measured in bits (e.g., 32-bit = 32 wires, 64-bit = 64 wires).

Bus Type Functions

Match each bus type to its function: memory bus, data bus, video bus, power bus.

Memory bus: CPU ↔ memory; Data bus: general data transfer; Video bus: CPU ↔ video system; Power bus: supplies power.

Form Factor Definition

What is a form factor in computer hardware?

The shape and size of a computer component.

Form Factor Types

How do proprietary and standardized form factors differ?

Proprietary: designed for a specific system and not interchangeable. Standardized: can fit a wide range of cases.

CPU and Memory Compatibility

Why must the CPU and memory match the motherboard?

Each motherboard supports only specific CPUs and memory types.

Compatibility Example

Give the example from the content of CPU and memory compatibility.

An MSI Tomahawk motherboard with an AM4 socket supports AMD Ryzen CPUs and specific DDR4 modules (e.g., DDR4-4800), not Intel CPUs.

Devices With Motherboards

Name at least four types of devices (besides desktop PCs) that contain motherboards.

Laptops, tablets, smartphones, consoles (also cars, appliances, TVs, phones).

ATX Sizes

List the ATX-related standardized desktop motherboard sizes given.

Standard-ATX (12" × 13"), Micro-ATX (9.6" × 9.6"), Mini-ATX (6.7" × 6.7"), Nano-ATX (4.7" × 4.7").

Standard Motherboard Replacement

What determines whether a standardized ATX motherboard can replace another?

It must physically fit the case and meet size, CPU support, RAM support, expansion, and power requirements.

Proprietary Desktop Limitation

Who commonly uses proprietary desktop motherboards and what is the limitation?

Companies like Dell, Apple, HP; replacement requires a specific model with few upgrade options.

Laptop Motherboards

Why are laptop motherboards not easily replaced or upgraded?

They use proprietary designs with components (including CPU) often soldered on.

Tablet Motherboards

What typically happens when a tablet motherboard component fails?

Components are soldered and hard to replace, so the device is usually discarded.

Smartphone Motherboards

What is the common repair outcome for smartphone motherboard issues?

They are hard to repair due to soldered parts, often leading to disposal.

Port vs Connector

What is the difference between a port and a connector?

A port is the hole or slot; a connector is the plug, jack, or card edge that fits into it.

CPU Socket

What does the CPU socket do on a motherboard?

Holds the processor and supports only specific brands/models.

CPU Power Connector

What are the two common CPU power connector types?

4-pin or 8-pin connectors.

Main Power Connector

What are the two common main power connector sizes?

20-pin and 24-pin.

SATA Ports

What do SATA ports connect to?

Storage devices like hard drives and DVD drives.

USB Headers

What are USB header ports used for?

Connecting external USB ports via 9-pin internal connectors.

Front Panel Connectors

What do front panel connectors attach to?

Power switch, hard drive activity light, and reset button.

Audio Connector

What is the (green) audio connector for?

Case audio ports such as a headphone jack.

PCIe ×1 Uses

Give examples of devices for a PCI Express ×1 slot.

Sound cards, network cards, video cards, etc.

PCIe ×16 Purpose

What is the purpose of a PCI Express ×16 slot?

It is used for video cards (and there may be multiple).

Legacy PCI Slot

What characterizes a legacy PCI slot?

Older design with more pin slots than PCI Express.

Fan Connectors

Why are fan connectors important?

They power cooling fans, essential to prevent overheating.

Sound Ports Variation

What variation may appear among sound ports on the backplane?

They vary by motherboard and may include a fiber optic audio port (gray, odd-shaped).

USB Port Variations

What is notable about USB ports on the backplane?

They are the most common ports; different colors indicate different standards; includes USB-C.

Ethernet Port

What does the Ethernet port look like and what is it for?

A square port used to connect the computer to a network.

Video Ports

Name the two video port types listed on the backplane and their usage context.

HDMI (widely used with monitors and TVs) and DisplayPort (less popular but common on some monitors).
