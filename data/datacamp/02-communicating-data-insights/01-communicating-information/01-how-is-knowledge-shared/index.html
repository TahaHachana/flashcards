The Four Types of Communication

What are the four types of communication that adept communicators blend to create impactful messages?

  1. Written — text on a page or in a presentation
  2. Visual — charts, graphs, pictures, and videos
  3. Nonverbal — facial expressions, posture, and eye contact
  4. Verbal — anything spoken and heard

Written Communication Definition

What is written communication, and what are its two key concepts for success?

Written communication is any information conveyed in text form (e.g., on a page or in a presentation).

Its two keys to success are: 1. Appropriate message format — matching the structure and delivery to audience expectations 2. Conciseness — being brief while retaining all essential supporting details

Message Format in Written Communication

Why is choosing the correct message format important in written communication? Give one example of an incorrect format.

Using the correct format removes a large barrier to sharing information by meeting audience expectations for standard structure and delivery.

Examples of incorrect format: - Writing a wall of incomprehensible text to answer a simple message - Writing a single paragraph to describe a complex business analysis

Conciseness in Written Communication

What does being concise in written communication require, and what is the main challenge it presents?

Being concise requires: - Having a clear statement in mind - Understanding what supporting information is needed to understand and appreciate that statement

The main challenge is striking a balance between over-explaining and leaving out important details.

Visual Communication Definition

What is visual communication, and why is it the most popular form of communication in the digital era?

Visual communication is information presented through pictures, videos, GIFs, charts, graphs, and infographics.

It is the most popular form in the digital era because it is: - Much easier to digest than written communication - Able to reach a wider audience

Keys to Visual Communication Success

What two principles drive success in visual communication, and how do they differ from their application in written communication?

The same two principles apply — conciseness and format — but they work differently:

  • Conciseness → fewer visual elements and fewer distractions, leading to an easier-to-understand message
  • Format → harder to choose than in written communication because there are expansive guidelines and best practices for each visual method

Pairing Visual and Written Communication

How do visual and written communication complement each other when used together?

Visual communication is an effective hook — it grabs the audience's attention quickly.

Following it up with written communication delivers a deeper, more detailed message.

Together they combine attention-grabbing power with substantive content.

Verbal Communication Keys to Success

What are the basic keys to success in verbal communication, and what advanced strength sets it apart from other forms?

Basic keys: 1. Choosing the right tone for your audience 2. Keeping a consistent message

Advanced strength: Flexibility — the ability to alter delivery, highlight new areas, and connect with the audience in real time, something not possible with written or visual communication.

Common Pitfall in Verbal Communication

What is a common mistake new communicators make when delivering a verbal message, and what does it cost the audience?

Many new communicators follow a rigid script and are not prepared to pivot or adapt.

This deprives the audience of the greatest strength of verbal communication: real-time flexibility — the ability to adjust delivery, emphasize different areas, and connect authentically in the moment.

Nonverbal Communication Definition

What is nonverbal communication, and what are three concrete examples of how it manifests in an audience?

Nonverbal communication encompasses physical and behavioral cues that signal how well a message is landing and whether the audience is engaged.

Three examples: 1. Facial expressions 2. Posture 3. Eye contact

Role of Nonverbal Communication

What is the primary function of nonverbal communication from a speaker's perspective?

Nonverbal communication is the main avenue for gathering feedback during a presentation or conversation.

It shows the speaker: - How well the message is performing - Whether the audience is engaged or if the message is missing the mark

This feedback loop is what enables the real-time adaptation that makes verbal communication powerful.
