Yank And Put

Which two commands together perform copy and paste in Vim?

y (yank) copies text into a register; p/P (put) places it back into the buffer.

p Versus P

What is the difference between p and P?

p puts text after the cursor / below the current line; P puts it before the cursor / above the current line.

Yank A Whole Line

How do you yank the entire current line, and how do you yank four lines?

yy (or Y) yanks the whole current line; 4yy yanks four lines.

The Timing Rule

After deleting or yanking into the unnamed register, when must you put the text back?

Before your next change or delete command — the next deletion or yank overwrites the unnamed register, so the previous contents are lost.

Repeating A Put

Can you put the same yanked text multiple times?

Yes — you can repeat p over and over, as long as you don't make a new edit in between (which would overwrite the register).

Transpose Two Characters

What command sequence transposes two characters, and what is the mnemonic?

xp — x deletes the character, p puts it after the cursor. Mnemonic: trans-pose, where x = trans and p = pose. (mvoe → cursor on v → xp → move.)

Transpose Two Lines

What does ddp do?

It swaps the current line with the line below it: dd cuts the line, p puts it back below the (now-current) next line.

Duplicate A Line

How do you duplicate the current line below it?

yy then p — yank the line (leaving the original) and put a copy below.

Cut And Paste Lines

Describe the keystroke flow to move a line elsewhere in the file.

dd to cut the line, move the cursor to the destination, then p (put below) or P (put above).

Transposing Words

Is there a single built-in command to transpose two words?

No — there is no built-in word-transpose command; it requires a short custom mapping.

Yank To End Of Line

How do you yank from the cursor to the end of the line (not the whole line)?

y$ — since Y yanks the whole line rather than to end of line.
