The General Command Form

What is the general syntactic form of a vi/Vim editing command?

[n] operator [m] motion — an optional count, an operator (what to do), and a motion (where it reaches). The operator acts on exactly the text the motion covers.

The Three Operators

Name the three basic editing operators and what each begins.

c = change (delete then enter insert mode), d = delete, y = yank (copy).

Doubling The Operator

What happens when you double an operator letter, e.g. dd, yy, cc?

It operates on the whole current line, regardless of cursor position: dd deletes the line, yy yanks the line, cc changes the line.

Combining Two Counts

If both counts are given as in 2d3w, how many objects are affected?

They multiply: n × m. 2d3w deletes 6 words.

Change Up To A Character

What does cf. do?

Changes text from the cursor up to and including the next period (f. is the motion: find the next .).

Delete Two Words (Cloze)

Cloze: To delete the next two words you can type either _____ (count after operator) or 2dw (count before operator).

d2w

D And C Shortcuts

What do D and C do, and what longer commands are they equivalent to?

D deletes from the cursor to end of line (= d$); C changes from the cursor to end of line (= c$).

The Y Inconsistency

Why is Y the odd one out compared with D and C?

Y does NOT yank to end of line — it yanks the whole line (same as yy). To yank from the cursor to end of line, use y$.

Single-Character Edits

What do x, X, and r do?

x deletes the character under the cursor; X deletes the character before it; r replaces a single character (no insert mode, no ESC needed). Prefix with a count, e.g. 5x.

s And S

What is the difference between s and S?

s substitutes character(s) — deletes them and enters insert mode (like r but with insert). S substitutes whole line(s), clearing the line and starting insert at its beginning (≈ cc).

cw Versus cc Behavior

In original vi, how does a sub-line change (cw) behave differently from a whole-line change (cc)?

cw marks the end of the affected text with $ and lets you type over the old text. cc wipes the line first, leaving a blank line to insert on. (In Vim's nocompatible mode, both just delete and enter insert mode.)

Motions As Targets

Give the effect of d}, cG, and d^.

d} deletes up to the next paragraph; cG changes to the end of the buffer; d^ deletes back to the first non-blank character of the line.

Search As A Motion

Can a pattern search be used as the motion target of an operator?

Yes. For example d/foo deletes from the cursor up to the next match of foo. Any movement command, including searches, can serve as the motion.
