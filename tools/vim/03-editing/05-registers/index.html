What A Register Is

What is a register in Vim?

A named storage slot for text. Deletes and yanks store text into a register; puts retrieve it. (Older vi docs call it the "deletion buffer.")

The Unnamed Register

Where does an ordinary delete or yank go by default, and what's the catch?

Into the unnamed register, which p/P reads from. The catch: every new delete or yank overwrites it, so you must put before the next edit.

Numbered Deletion Registers

What do numbered registers 1–9 hold, and which is which?

The last nine deletions. Register 1 is the most recent deletion, 2 the second-most-recent, and so on through 9.

Numbered Register Caveat

Which deletions are NOT saved in the numbered registers?

Small deletions of less than a line (single words or partial lines). Only deletions of a line or more are stored; small ones can only be recovered with p/P immediately after deleting.

Recover A Past Deletion (Cloze)

Cloze: To put the second-to-last deletion after the cursor, type _____.

"2p

Yank Into A Named Register

How do you yank the current line into register d, and later put it before the cursor?

"dyy to yank into register d; "dP to put its contents before the cursor.

Named Register Lifespan

Why use a named register (a–z) instead of relying on the unnamed register?

Named registers keep their contents for the whole editing session, so they survive intervening edits — whereas the unnamed register is overwritten by the next delete or yank.

Uppercase Register Appends

What happens when you yank or delete into an uppercase register name like "Z?

It appends to the matching lowercase register (z) instead of overwriting it, letting you accumulate several pieces of text into one register.

Append Register Warning

What is the danger when accumulating text with an uppercase (append) register?

If you forget the capital once and yank/delete to the lowercase name, you overwrite the register and lose everything you had accumulated.

Register Command Syntax

What is the general syntax for directing a command at a specific register (the "bcommand form)?

"{register}{command} — a double quote, the register name, then the command. E.g. "bdd deletes a line into register b, and "bp puts it back.

Registers Across Files

Are named registers cleared when you load a new file with :e?

No. Named registers persist across :e, so you can yank text, switch files, and put it into the new file. They're shared across windows in one session but not between separate Vim sessions.
