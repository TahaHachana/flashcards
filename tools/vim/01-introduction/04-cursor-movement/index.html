The hjkl Directions

What direction does each of h, j, k, and l move the cursor?

h   Left one space
j   Down one line
k   Up one line
l   Right one space

Mode Required For Movement

In which mode do h j k l work, and what should you do before using them?

Command mode. Press ESC first to be sure you're in command mode; otherwise the keys would just type their letters into the file.

Remembering h And l

Which of h/l moves left and which moves right?

h (leftmost of the four) moves left; l (rightmost) moves right.

Behavior At An Edge

What happens when you try to move past the limit in a direction?

The editor beeps and the cursor stops; it will not move further in that direction.

h And l Do Not Wrap Lines

Can h or l move from the end of one line to another line?

No. At the start or end of a line, h and l do not wrap to the previous/next line — use j or k to change lines. (With nocompatible, l/space can sometimes space past the end of a line.)

Where The Cursor Cannot Go

Where can the cursor not be moved with these basic motions?

Onto a tilde (~) line (a non-existent line below the text) or above the first line of the file.

Why Letters Not Arrows

Why were letter keys (h j k l) used for movement instead of arrow keys?

vi was built on the Lear-Siegler ADM-3A terminal, which had no arrow keys (arrows were painted on the H J K L keys). Different terminals' arrow keys also sent conflicting escape codes starting with ESC, so letter motions avoided the ambiguity.

The Home-Row Advantage

What is the practical advantage of h j k l that touch typists insisted on keeping?

You navigate without taking your fingers off the center (home row) of the keyboard, rather than reaching for the arrow keys.

Alternate Single Movements

Name some alternate keys for single-step movement (and why they're discouraged).

Arrow keys, + and - (down/up a line), CTRL-N (down) and CTRL-P (up), ENTER (down), BACKSPACE (left). They work but are "out of the way" compared to h j k l.

Cloze — j And k

{{j}} moves the cursor down one line and {{k}} moves it up one line.
